Section 1 — Representation

The U.S. House of Representatives is composed of Members elected from districts apportioned among the several States according to their respective populations.

A congressional district containing more than one hundred thousand persons, as determined by the most recent decennial census, is invalid. An election conducted from an invalid district has no legal effect.

Section 2 — Lawful Places of Assembly

Congress may by law designate additional lawful places of assembly for the House of Representatives.

Proceedings conducted at any lawful place of assembly possess the same constitutional force and effect as proceedings conducted at the seat of government.

Section 3 — Equality of Legislative Authority

The legislative authority of a Member of the House of Representatives does not depend upon physical presence at any particular place of assembly.

Any rule or practice that assigns unequal voting power based on physical location is void.

Section 4 — Enforcement

This article is self-executing. Any person with standing under Article III may seek judicial relief to enforce its provisions.
